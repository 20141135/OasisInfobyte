Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, was a renowned Indian scientist, engineer, and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Widely known as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India," Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of India's space and missile programs. His illustrious career began at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and later continued at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO). Notable for his leadership in the successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, Kalam received widespread acclaim for his contributions to India's technological advancements.

Beyond his scientific achievements, he was a charismatic and inspirational figure, actively engaging with students and youth, promoting education, and advocating for societal development. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving behind a legacy of scientific excellence and a commitment to the betterment of society.
